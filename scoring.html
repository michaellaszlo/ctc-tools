Proposed ranking method

Each team carries a total number of points from month to month. This total is adjusted according to the number of boats the team has floated in the current month.

Let x be the number of boats floated by a team. Then:

  • • If x is greater than zero, the team gains floor(100·log2(x+1)) points. ▶ What does this formula mean?

    • The scoring formula awards many points for the first few boats floated by a team in one month. As a team floats more boats, the number of points for each additional boat becomes progressively smaller. This means that teams still have an incentive to float more points, but large teams will not earn vastly more points than small teams.

  • • If x is zero, the team loses 20% of its total.

Teams are ranked according to their total. ▶ How are ties broken?

Ties for first place should occur rarely. With this formula, there are no ties for the past nine years of CTC data.

If there is a tie for first place, it is broken according to the following rules:

  • • A team that has never chosen a challenge is preferred.
  • • Otherwise, a team that chose its last challenge longer ago is preferred.

  • • If there is still a tie, the winner is chosen randomly.

The team in first place wins the right to select the next challenge. At the start of the following month, its score is reset to half the number of points it earned in the current month.
